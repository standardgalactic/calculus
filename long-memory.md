## 2. The Choice Of Locality: What Formulas Forget

Classical mathematics relies on locality: the idea that change at time `t` depends only on an infinitesimal neighborhood around `t`.

This appears in the derivative:
```
K(t, τ) = δ(t − τ)
```

This assumption has three consequences:

- **Tractability**: equations become solvable  
- **Amnesia**: the past is erased  
- **Artificial Memory**: hidden variables are introduced to simulate history  

Real systems—biological, physical, economic—do not obey this restriction.
