# The Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum (RSVP)  
## Irreversibility, Identity, and the Geometry of History

The RSVP framework proposes a shift in how systems are understood:

from static states  

to dynamic histories  

---

At its core is a simple claim:

> A system is not what it is.  
> It is what it has survived.

---

## The Framework

Reality is modeled as a coupled field:

X = (Φ, v, S)

where:

Φ — scalar coherence (structure, meaning)  
v — vector transport (movement, propagation)  
S — entropy (contradiction, obstruction)  

These fields interact through a cascade:

S → v → Φ  

---

## Central Thesis

Irreversibility is not a secondary effect of physics.

It is a **structural consequence of non-invertible reconstruction**.

---

### Key Mechanism

A mapping from reality to history:

F̃ : X → H  

is not invertible.

---

### Reconstruction

G : H → X  

provides the best possible recovery.

---

### Defect

Δ(X) = ‖X − G(F̃(X))‖  

---

### Interpretation

The arrow of time emerges from:

information lost in projection
