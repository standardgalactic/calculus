## 1. Introduction: Beyond Static Systems

Traditional models treat systems as static snapshots—a set of variables frozen in time.

The RSVP framework rejects this.

A system is not a fixed configuration but a **dynamic triple**:

X = (Φ, v, S)

that evolves through continuous cycles of conflict and resolution.

---

### Why Fields?

The key reason is **irreversibility**.

Systems cannot simply undo their past.

This is not just a feature of time—it is structural.

---

### The Reconstruction Defect

Δ = loss of recoverable history  

When moving from past → present, information is compressed.

This loss cannot be reversed.

---

### Three Core Questions

1. What forces irreversibility?  
   → Non-invertibility of history  

2. How does loss occur?  
   → Projection from complexity to history  

3. Why does history matter?  
   → Identity is path-dependent  

---

### Key Insight

Two systems may appear identical now,

but differ fundamentally due to their histories.
